# SmartCities
Building Cities for the Future 

AI and technology are reshaping four cities that represent four different urban worlds: Bangalore, New York, Newark, and Dubai. The goal of this is to trace each city's trajectory from its early smart-city experiments to its current reality to its committed future plans. Across all four, the same pattern emerges: grand platform-scale "smart city" visions largely failed, while narrow AI tools solving specific problems such as permits, construction, energy, inspections are quietly succeeding. Each city's progress is shaped by its own governance, money, and climate. The comparison reveals both universal yet to be solved and city-specific problems, pointing to solutions where the next generation of Urban AI will be built.
